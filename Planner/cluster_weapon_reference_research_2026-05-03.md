# Cluster Weapon Reference Research - 2026-05-03

## Purpose

The user asked what kinds of cluster-targeting weapons appear in Survivor.io, Vampire Survivors, and similar survivor-like clone projects. This note summarizes reference patterns that can inform BangBang Survivor weapon planning.

Cluster-targeting means a weapon is useful when many enemies gather together. It can do this by damaging an area, piercing through many enemies, blocking a lane, or protecting the player with a nearby damaging field.

## Main Reference Patterns

### 1. Player-Centered Aura

Examples:

- Vampire Survivors: Garlic
- Survivor.io: Forcefield

Pattern:

- A circular damage zone follows the player.
- It damages enemies that enter or touch the circle.
- It is simple to understand and strong against weak enemies that crowd the player.

Design use:

- Best for beginner-friendly defensive weapons.
- Good when the player feels trapped.
- Risk: if too strong, the player can stand still and ignore movement.

BangBang Survivor fit:

- Works well as a school-themed "whistle shockwave", "chalk dust cloud", or "smelly lunch aura" idea.

### 2. Orbiting Barrier

Examples:

- Vampire Survivors: King Bible
- Survivor.io: Guardian

Pattern:

- Objects rotate around the player.
- They form a partial barrier and repeatedly hit nearby enemies.
- They can also create spacing through knockback or repeated contact damage.

Design use:

- Good for close-range survival.
- More readable than an invisible aura because the player can see the rotating objects.
- Risk: too many orbiting objects can visually clutter the screen.

BangBang Survivor fit:

- Current school bag swing is already close to this role.
- Future variants could use rotating rulers, notebooks, or lunch trays.

### 3. Ground Damage Zone

Examples:

- Vampire Survivors: Santa Water, La Borra
- Survivor.io: Molotov, Fuel Barrel, Inferno Bomb

Pattern:

- Creates a burning, puddle, explosion, or lingering hazard area on the ground.
- Enemies take damage while standing in it.
- Strong against dense groups that naturally walk through the same space.

Design use:

- Excellent for group damage.
- Gives the player a tactical feeling: lure enemies into the zone.
- Risk: overlaps heavily with BangBang Survivor's science flask if the new weapon is also just a thrown puddle.

BangBang Survivor fit:

- Science flask already owns this role.
- New weapons should only use this pattern if they differ clearly, such as a moving zone or player-centered zone.

### 4. Bouncing or Piercing Projectile

Examples:

- Vampire Survivors: Runetracer, Death Spiral
- Survivor.io: Drill Shot, Durian, Soccer Ball, Brick / One Ton Iron

Pattern:

- A projectile travels through enemies, bounces around, or crosses the screen.
- It can hit many enemies in one cast.
- It becomes stronger in tight maps because bouncing gives more repeated hits.

Design use:

- Good for opening paths through a crowd.
- Feels active and energetic without requiring manual aiming.
- Risk: random bouncing can feel unreliable on open fields.

BangBang Survivor fit:

- Strong fit for "rolling lunch tray", "thrown eraser", or "runaway sports ball".

### 5. Random Area Strike

Examples:

- Vampire Survivors: Lightning Ring
- Survivor.io: Lightning Emitter, Supercell

Pattern:

- The weapon strikes random enemies or random enemy areas.
- Upgrades increase strike count, range, or shockwave size.
- Useful when there are many enemies because random targeting is more likely to hit something.

Design use:

- Good for screen pressure and passive damage.
- Easy for the player because targeting is automatic.
- Risk: can feel unfair or unsatisfying if it misses the group the player cares about.

BangBang Survivor fit:

- Could become "static shock from school broadcast speaker" or "faulty lab coil".

### 6. Mine or Trap

Examples:

- Survivor.io: Modular Mine, Inferno Bomb, Thunderbolt Bomb

Pattern:

- Mines are placed randomly or near enemies.
- They explode when enemies approach or after a delay.
- Strong against groups because many enemies can trigger one explosion.

Design use:

- Good for delayed area control.
- Makes movement and kiting matter.
- Risk: if placement is too random, player cannot read or plan around it.

BangBang Survivor fit:

- Possible school version: "floor wax puddle", "science kit mine", or "dropped marble trap".

### 7. Drone or Turret

Examples:

- Survivor.io: Drone-A, Drone-B, Destroyer

Pattern:

- A companion or turret repeatedly fires at enemies.
- It can cover both boss and mob situations depending on targeting.
- Usually scales by missile count, rate, or targeting quality.

Design use:

- Strong general-purpose option.
- Less specifically "cluster" unless missiles splash or fire rapidly.
- Risk: can overlap with basic gun if it only shoots single targets.

BangBang Survivor fit:

- Could be "toy drone", "teacher's pointer turret", or "school broadcast drone", but it should have splash or multi-shot to solve clustering.

## Useful Takeaways For BangBang Survivor

The most relevant anti-cluster patterns for the current problem are:

1. Player-centered aura or pulse
   - Directly solves "zombies are already gathered on me."
   - Simple to implement and easy for the player to understand.

2. Bouncing or piercing path opener
   - Solves "I need to cut through a crowd."
   - Distinct from science flask.

3. Trap or mine
   - Solves "a group is approaching through the same path."
   - Distinct if it is placed defensively instead of thrown at the densest group.

The less urgent patterns are:

- Random lightning: useful, but less controllable.
- Drone/turret: useful, but can become a general DPS weapon rather than a cluster answer.
- Another ground puddle: powerful, but overlaps with science flask.
